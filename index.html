About

I am a Research Fellow in Health Text Analytics and Data Science within the Department of Biostatistics & Health Informatics (BHI) at King’s College London. My research focuses on applying advanced artificial intelligence techniques to address healthcare challenges, with a particular emphasis on mental health. I aim to develop innovative AI-driven solutions that integrate data science and health research to improve patient outcomes. In addition to my primary role, I serve as an Advisor to the Meta Global Eating Disorder and Body Image Advisory Group, a Member of the King’s Institute for Artificial Intelligence, Honorary Staff at South London and Maudsley NHS Foundation Trust, and Co-chair of the NLP Interest Group at BHI.

Research

Motivated by a deep passion for transforming healthcare through technology, my research sits at the intersection of artificial intelligence, health data science, and clinical informatics. I focus on developing cutting-edge computational methods to tackle real-world healthcare challenges, with a strong emphasis on advancing outcomes in complex mental health. By leveraging diverse data sources—from clinical records to social media—I use natural language processing and multimodal learning to uncover meaningful patterns in human behavior and health outcomes. My work has led to actionable insights for population health management and the development of clinical decision support tools that drive better care and patient outcomes. My research interests include:

  • Natural Language Processing
  • Health Data Science
  • Multimodal Learning in Healthcare Applications
  • Knowledge Graphs for Medical Information
  • Clinical Decision Support Systems
