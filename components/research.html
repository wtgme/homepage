Research Interests

  • Clinical Natural Language Processing: Extracting meaningful information from unstructured clinical text, developing methods for timeline extraction, and creating models for understanding clinical narratives
  • Health Data Science: Using advanced analytics to derive insights from large-scale healthcare datasets, including electronic health records and clinical notes
  • Multimodal Learning: Combining different data modalities (text, structured data, images) for comprehensive healthcare applications
  • Knowledge Graphs: Building and utilizing medical knowledge graphs to enhance reasoning and inference in clinical applications
  • Health Informatics: Developing tools and systems to support clinical decision-making and improve healthcare delivery
  • Mental Health Analytics: Applying AI to understand, monitor, and improve mental healthcare services and outcomes

Current Research Projects

VIEWER Framework: Visual Analytics for Mental Healthcare

VIEWER is an extensible visual analytics framework for enhancing mental healthcare delivery through population health management. This project combines natural language processing, data visualization, and clinical expertise to create a tool that enables clinicians to monitor care quality, identify trends, and address health inequalities.

Key achievements:

  • Development of a real-time dashboard for monitoring care quality across diverse mental health services
  • Integration of NLP capabilities to extract meaningful insights from unstructured clinical notes
  • Deployment in clinical settings with demonstrated impact on service improvement
  • Award-winning platform recognized for its innovative approach to healthcare analytics

ChronoMed: Medical Timeline Extraction

ChronoMed is a medical timeline builder that extracts clinical events from electronic health records using large language models (LLMs). This project addresses the critical need to reconstruct patient journeys from unstructured clinical documentation, providing clinicians with a comprehensive view of a patient's medical history.

Key features:

  • Implementation of advanced LLMs for temporal information extraction
  • Development of specialized prompting techniques for clinical text analysis
  • Creation of visualization tools for intuitive timeline representation
  • Integration with existing clinical systems for seamless workflow

Improving Identification of Treatment-Resistant Schizophrenia

This project utilizes generative AI to better identify and understand treatment resistance patterns in schizophrenia. By analyzing complex clinical data, including medication histories, symptom profiles, and treatment responses, we aim to develop better predictive models for treatment resistance.

Research goals:

  • Development of NLP models to extract medication information and treatment response indicators
  • Creation of predictive algorithms to identify patients at risk of treatment resistance
  • Analysis of clinical pathways to understand factors contributing to treatment resistance
  • Implementation of decision support tools to guide clinical interventions
